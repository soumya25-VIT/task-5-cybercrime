# task-5-cybercrime
Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud
UPI or online payment fraud is a common cybercrime where people are tricked into sending money or sharing sensitive banking details. With the rapid growth of digital payments in India, such frauds have become more frequent, as noted by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI).
In a typical situation, a person selling an item online receives a call from someone pretending to be a buyer. The fraudster sends a payment request and explains that entering the UPI PIN is necessary to receive money. Trusting this, the victim enters the PIN, but instead of receiving money, the amount is deducted from their account. In other cases, scammers may send fake links, ask for OTPs, or impersonate bank officials to gain trust and access accounts.
Students, online sellers, and people who are not fully aware of how digital payment systems work are the most common targets. These frauds can lead to financial loss, stress, and a loss of trust in online transactions. Recovering the lost money is often difficult.
To prevent such incidents, users should never share their UPI PIN or OTP and must carefully verify any payment request. Awareness and caution are key to avoiding such cybercrimes.
Stay Safe Online
Things to Do
•	Try to use strong and different passwords for all your accounts instead of using the same one everywhere 
•	Turn on two-factor authentication (2FA) for apps like email, social media, and banking for extra security 
•	Be careful about what you share online, especially personal details like your phone number or address 
•	Think twice before clicking on unknown links or downloading anything from untrusted sources 
•	Always double-check messages or calls that ask for your details—they might not be genuine 
 For Financial / UPI Safety
•	Never share your UPI PIN or OTP with anyone, even if they sound official 
•	Don’t accept random “collect requests” unless you are sure you are making a payment 
•	Before sending money, take a moment to check the name and UPI ID of the receiver 
 General Awareness
•	Avoid using public Wi-Fi for things like payments or logging into important accounts 
•	If you use a shared device, make sure to log out and don’t save your passwords 
 If Something Goes Wrong
•	You can report cybercrime at: National Cyber Crime Portal – cybercrime.gov.in 
•	Or call the helpline number: 1930
